Alan Turing


The father of theoretical computer science and AI


Alan Turing Life Timeline

  • 1923 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1931 - Entered University of Cambridge to study mathematics
  • 1935 - Elected a fellow of King's College in recognition of his research in probability theory
  • 1936 - Published seminal paper "On Compatible Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem (Decision Problem)" which has profound signifigance for the emerging science of computing
  • 1936 - In the course of his paper, invents the universal Turing Machine, an abstract machine that would be capable of performing any conceivable mathematical computation if it were representable as an algorithm
  • 1938 - Completed Ph.D in mathematical logic at Princeton University, writes dissertation where he introduced the concept of ordinal logic and the notion of relative computing
  • 1938 - Worked part time with the Government Code and Cypher School, a british codebreaking organization
  • 1940 - Helped design codebreaking machine known as the Bomba, which supplied allies with large amount of military intelligence
  • 1942 - Devised first systematic method for breaking messages encrypted by the German cypher machine called "Tunny"
  • 1946 - Made an Officer of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire for his work on code breaking during the war
  • 1948 - Helped create first chess computer game ever to enter development
  • 1950 - Wrote paper in which he proposed the Turing test, which was an attempt to define a standard for computing machines and artificial intelligence
  • 1951 - Wrote the first ever programming manual for the first marketable electronic digital computer, the Ferranti Mark 1
  • 1952 - Convicted of British crime of homosexuality, sentenced to 12 months of hormone therapy
  • 1954 - Dies at home from cyanide posioning, unclear if death was suicide or accidental posioning
